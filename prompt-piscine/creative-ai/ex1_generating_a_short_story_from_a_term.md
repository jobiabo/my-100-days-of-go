# Exercise 1: Generating a Short Story from a Theme
#### Objective: Practice creative writing through prompts.

### Model used :
  - [OpenAI](https://www.chatgpt.com)

- **Story Theme:** Space Exploration.

* **Prompt 1 input:** Write a short story about Space Exploration in under 300 words.
* **Model Output:** The first human footsteps on Mars did not belong to a hero.

They belonged to Mira Ionescu, propulsion engineer, backup crew member, professional overthinker.

When the *Astraea* descended through the copper haze, Mira watched the planet rise to meet them—rusted plains stretching toward the horizon, dust devils twisting like curious ghosts. Earth was a blue star now, trembling in the black.

“Touchdown confirmed,” said Commander Alvarez.

Mira wasn’t scheduled to step out first. But when a faulty seal jammed the commander’s suit, procedures changed. Space exploration, she realized, wasn’t about destiny. It was about whoever could solve the next problem.

The airlock cycled open.

Mars smelled like nothing at all—her suit filters humming softly. She climbed down the ladder, boots trembling against metal rungs. For a moment she imagined the countless dreamers who had pointed telescopes skyward: Galileo Galilei sketching moons by candlelight, Valentina Tereshkova orbiting Earth with a quiet heartbeat, the engineers of Apollo 11 calculating trajectories with slide rules and stubborn hope.

“Surface contact,” she whispered.

Her boot pressed into the dust, leaving a print sharp as a signature.

In that instant, Mars stopped being a distant red dot and became a place—cold, silent, real. A world that would remember them in footprints long after wind erased their names.

Back inside the habitat module hours later, Mira transmitted the first geological scans. Ice beneath the soil. Resources. Possibility.

Exploration, she realized, wasn’t conquest. It was conversation.

And Mars, patient for billions of years, had finally answered.



* **Prompt 2 input:** Write a short story about Overcoming fear in under 300 words.
* **Model Output:** The first time Jonah tried to ride a bike, he studied the pavement like it had personally offended him.

“It’s just balance,” his sister said, holding the seat.

“It’s just falling,” Jonah muttered.

Fear, he’d decided, was practical. Pavement scraped. Gravity never forgot its job. He preferred sidewalks, helmets, and solid plans.

But this summer was different. His sister was leaving for college, and the bike was hers. The paint was chipped blue, the handles wrapped in tape like bandaged fists. “You can keep it,” she’d said. “Just promise you’ll actually ride it.”

So there he was, heart banging louder than the cicadas.

She pushed.

For three glorious seconds, Jonah flew.

Then gravity remembered him.

He hit the ground in a tangle of limbs and pride. His knee burned. His eyes stung worse.

“Again,” his sister said gently.

Fear grew louder. It listed evidence: pain, embarrassment, the certainty of another fall. It painted vivid, humiliating futures.

But beneath the noise was something quieter. A flicker. Three seconds of flying.

“Again,” Jonah said.

They tried until the sun melted into orange streaks. Each fall hurt less. Each glide lasted longer. His sister’s hand rested lighter on the seat.

And then—without fanfare, without announcement—her hand wasn’t there at all.

Jonah pedaled.

The houses blurred. The wind pressed against his cheeks like applause. He was moving under his own power, wobbling but unstoppable.

He laughed, sudden and wild.

Fear hadn’t vanished. It trailed behind him, small and breathless. But it no longer steered.

When he circled back, his sister was standing in the street, hands cupped around her mouth.

“You’re doing it!”

Jonah grinned, turning toward open road.

He was.


#### Brief:
Two examples was used in this exercise and the model was allowed to generate a short from any source but the constraint is the model has give an output of text not more than 300 words.
