**Climate change** refers to statistically significant, long-term alterations in the mean state and variability of the Earth’s climate system, persisting for decades or longer. It encompasses changes in temperature, precipitation patterns, atmospheric circulation, ocean dynamics, and cryospheric extent.

---

###### 1️⃣ Physical Basis

Climate change is fundamentally governed by **Earth’s radiative energy balance**. The planet maintains equilibrium when incoming solar radiation equals outgoing terrestrial (infrared) radiation.

Anthropogenic increases in greenhouse gases (GHGs) — primarily carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and nitrous oxide (N₂O) — enhance atmospheric infrared absorption, producing **positive radiative forcing**. This reduces outgoing longwave radiation to space and results in net warming of the lower atmosphere and surface.

Measured CO₂ concentrations have risen from ~280 ppm (pre-industrial) to over 420 ppm, largely due to fossil fuel combustion, cement production, and land-use change.

---

###### 2️⃣ Climate System Components Affected

Climate change involves coupled interactions among:

* **Atmosphere** – tropospheric warming, stratospheric cooling
* **Hydrosphere** – ocean heat uptake, thermal expansion
* **Cryosphere** – glacier retreat, Arctic sea ice decline
* **Lithosphere & Biosphere** – permafrost thaw, ecosystem shifts

Over 90% of excess heat is absorbed by the oceans, making ocean heat content a primary indicator of ongoing warming.

---

###### 3️⃣ Observed Trends

Empirical observations show:

* ~1.1–1.3°C increase in global mean surface temperature since the late 19th century
* Accelerated sea-level rise (~3–4 mm/year)
* Increased frequency/intensity of extreme heat events
* Altered precipitation regimes and hydrological cycles

These changes exceed natural variability and are attributed primarily to anthropogenic forcing using detection and attribution studies.

---

###### 4️⃣ Distinction from Natural Variability

Natural drivers include:

* Volcanic activity
* Solar irradiance variability
* Orbital (Milankovitch) cycles

However, current warming trends cannot be explained by these factors alone. Climate models incorporating anthropogenic forcing closely reproduce observed temperature trajectories.

---

###### 5️⃣ Scientific Definition

> Climate change is a persistent alteration in the statistical properties of the climate system driven primarily by anthropogenic radiative forcing resulting from increased greenhouse gas concentrations.
