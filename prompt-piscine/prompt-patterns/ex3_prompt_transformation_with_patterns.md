Climate change is the long-term change in Earth’s average temperature and weather patterns. While Earth’s climate has changed naturally in the past, today’s climate change is happening much faster because of human activities. The main cause is the release of greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide (CO₂) and methane into the atmosphere. These gases come from burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas for electricity, transportation, and industry. They trap heat in the atmosphere, making the planet warmer. This process is called the greenhouse effect.

As the Earth warms, we see effects such as melting glaciers, rising sea levels, stronger storms, heatwaves, droughts, and floods. These changes can affect food production, water supply, wildlife, and human health. For example, extreme heat can cause health problems, and heavy rains can destroy homes and farms.

Climate change matters because it impacts people, ecosystems, and economies around the world. However, there are solutions. We can reduce emissions by using renewable energy like solar and wind power, saving electricity, planting trees, and supporting environmental policies. Understanding climate change helps young people make informed choices and contribute to a more sustainable future.
